What is a Usability Test?

A Usability Test allows a developer to test how easily and effectively users can interact with and navigate the website.

Identify Usability Issues

A Usability Test allows a developer to test how easily and effectively users can interact with and navigate the website.

Gather User Feedback

1. Find the cheapest auto-motive antifreeze for RVs

Our test user was directed to find the cheapest auto-motive antifreeze for RVs. Surprisingly, the test user found this task a bit confusing as they first searched for RVs and then filtered by the lowest price ranges. The test user then sorted the products by price range from low to high to find the product. This task seemed relatively easy, however, our test user took surprisingly long to complete it, potentially indicating that some key words from the task may not have been captured in the product description.
Time: 69 seconds

2. Find the closest auto-repair shop using our location

This task was relatively easy. However, our test user seemed to not know where to go at first. When our test user found the yellow location icon at the top of our page, they were then easily able to accept the website location tracker and was then given the location of the closest shop. Our website location icon is able to capture the user’s attention because it is in the usual spot most websites would put their location. Additionally, the yellow location icon serves as a call to action and stood out.
Time: 22 seconds

3. Find step by step instructions on how to replace an accessory drive belt

This task was relatively simple for our test user to conduct. Our test user first navigated to the “Know How” section on our front page and was able to select the link to “Replacing a Belt” from the drop-down list. Although the test user was able to complete the task easily, other users may find it difficult if they were to skip the main page as the “Know How” drop-down list is a relatively small part of our busy front page.
Time: 24 seconds

4. Find the email and phone number to find a way to contact the company

After briefly looking at the bottom of NAPA’s main page, our test user decided to find the “About Us” page, believing that the contact information will be available there. Our test user initially thought that the “About Us” link would be in the nav bar on the left side of the page so, they open up drop-down menu and was then met with the “Contact Us” link at the bottom of the list. They then navigated towards the contact page of NAPA. Therefore, our test user didn’t get to the “About Us” page but, they took a shortcut to the contact page.
Time: 37 seconds

5. Find sun shades and place them in your shopping cart

Our test user was able to do this test relatively easily. Our user first searched up sun shades and was then able to be met with a selection of sun shades. They then chose the super jumbo sun shade and selected the “Add to Cart” button on the side of the product. The other way to access sun shades from our main page is to follow the “ALL PRODUCTS” drop-down list and then select the following: “ACCESSORIES”, “INTERIOR”, and then “SUN SHADES.” The user would then be met with the same page as if the user searched up sun shades in our search bar.
Time: 15 seconds
